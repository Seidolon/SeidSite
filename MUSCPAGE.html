What I Like

To start off, I will put here what kind of music I enjoy. I listen primarily to metal. My favorite bands are Amorphis, Children of Bodom, Death, Dissection, Sodom, Immortal, and Burzum. Other bands that I also like a lot are Metallica, Slayer, DRI, Misfits, Broken Bones, Charged GBH, Tool, Alice in Chains, Pantera, Ozzy Osbourne, Black Sabbath, and Led Zeppelin. I used to listen to a lot of 90's demoscene music as well, and while I don't listen to it as much nowadays, I still listen to some of it on occasion. There is no denying that there are many talented musicians who created music in the demoscene.







Resonating Skies

I created a Dark Ambient demo a while back using my guitar, my bass, my amps, various pedals, and several strange recorded sound effects. I did all the sound editing in Audacity, and while it isn't the nicest sounding demo, I still like it a lot.

The first song, Hypnotic Terror, is mostly comprised of sounds of me scraping my multi-tool across my guitar strings, then adding lots of echo in post production.

The next one, Incandescence features more technical guitar parts, and some improvised parts in the middle and end of the song.

Numinous is a recording of me improvising fingerstyle in one take, although there was at least one riff that I played before, so it's not entirely improvised.

The title track starts with me playing my bass and using a wah pedal and fuzz pedal, and adding lots of echo in post. There is also a reversed bassline playing in the background throughout the beginning.

The fifth track, Deep Down Under, starts off with a riff I came up with on the spot, and the background noise is the sound I recorded from my phone while on a subway, but slowed down and echoed. The guitar parts after that were all improvised.

Augmented Ions is me improvising with a very strange tuning on my guitar while also using a fuzz pedal and flanger pedal. The tuning used was (in order from low string to high.) F# A B F# B E

Waiting out the Storm is my favorite track. It is the first one I started, even though I finished Hypnotic Terror first, the same night in fact. I recorded some guitar screeches then pitch shifted and super echoed them to get that psychedelic thunder that plays the first couple of minutes. I have numerous strange samples in this song, from my sister's dog growling into my mic, to me cutting paper with scissors. The ending riff was recorded on the spot, and I was initially going to stop there, but I thought a cool wah solo would be good to go with it, so I recorded it in a single take, and applied the wah in post processing.
